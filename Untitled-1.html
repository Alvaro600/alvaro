Session 1: Self Introduction

Learning Objectives

  • Learn how to introduce yourself confidently in English
  • Understand the key components of a good self-introduction
  • Practice introducing yourself in different contexts
  • Develop proper pronunciation and body language

Learning Materials

Key Components

  1. Greeting: Start with "Hello" or "Hi"
  2. Name: "My name is..." or "I'm..."
  3. Age: "I'm 12 years old"
  4. School: "I study at..." or "I go to..."
  5. Hobbies: "I like..." or "I enjoy..."
  6. Closing: "Nice to meet you"

Example Introductions

Formal Introduction:

"Good morning. My name is David Wilson. I'm 13 years old and I study at Sunshine Junior High School. I enjoy reading books and playing football. Nice to meet you."

Casual Introduction:

"Hi! I'm Lisa. I'm 12 and I go to Greenfield Middle School. I love drawing and listening to music. Nice to meet you all!"

1. Complete the Introduction

Fill in the blanks to complete this introduction:

3. Record Yourself

Cultural Tip

In English-speaking countries, introductions often include a handshake and eye contact. Smiling is important!

Introduction Tips

  • Stand up straight when introducing yourself
  • Speak clearly at a moderate pace
  • Maintain eye contact
  • Keep it brief (30-60 seconds)
  • Practice until it feels natural

Pronunciation Guide

  • "Hello" - /həˈloʊ/ (huh-LOH)
  • "My name is" - /maɪ neɪm ɪz/ (my naym iz)
  • "Nice to meet you" - /naɪs tə mit ju/ (nyse tuh meet yoo)
  • Practice linking words: "Nice to meet you" → "Nice t'meet you"

Session 2: Daily Greetings

Learning Objectives

  • Learn appropriate greetings for different times of day
  • Understand formal and informal greeting situations
  • Practice responding to common greetings
  • Develop natural pronunciation and intonation

Learning Materials

Time-Based Greetings

  • Morning (5AM-12PM): "Good morning"
  • Afternoon (12PM-5PM): "Good afternoon"
  • Evening (5PM-9PM): "Good evening"
  • Night (after 9PM): "Good night" (only when leaving)

Situational Greetings

  • Formal: "Hello, Mr. Smith"
  • Informal: "Hi!", "Hey there!"
  • Friends: "What's up?", "How's it going?"
  • Phone: "Hello" (not time-based)

Example Dialogues

A

Good morning, Mrs. Johnson!

B

Good morning, Sarah! How are you today?

A

I'm doing well, thank you. How about you?

C

Hey Mike! What's up?

D

Not much, just heading to class. You?

Practice Activities

1. Matching Greetings

Match each situation with the appropriate greeting:

8:00 AM to teacher
"Good morning, Mr. Brown"
3:00 PM to friend
"Hey! What's up?"

2. Create Dialogues

Write short dialogues for these situations:

Situation 1:

Greeting your friend at 10:00 AM

Situation 2:

Greeting your teacher at 1:00 PM

3. Role Play

Practice these greetings with a partner:

Cultural Tip

In many English-speaking countries, greetings often include a handshake in formal situations or a wave/hug between friends.

Pronunciation Tips

  • "Good morning" often sounds like "G'morning"
  • "How are you?" → "How ya doin'?"
  • "What's up?" → "Wassup?"
  • Rising intonation makes greetings sound friendlier

Session 3: Farewells

Learning Objectives

  • Learn common English farewell expressions
  • Understand appropriate contexts for different goodbyes
  • Practice ending conversations politely
  • Develop natural-sounding farewell phrases

Learning Materials

Common Farewells

  • General: "Goodbye", "Bye", "Bye-bye"
  • See you again: "See you later", "See you", "See ya"
  • Time-specific: "See you tomorrow", "See you next week"
  • Wishing well: "Take care", "Have a nice day"
  • Nighttime: "Good night" (only when leaving/going to bed)

Situational Farewells

  • Formal: "Goodbye, Mr. Smith. Have a nice day."
  • Informal: "Bye!", "Later!", "Catch you later"
  • Friends: "Peace", "Later gator" (very casual)
  • Phone: "Bye for now", "Talk to you soon"

Example Dialogues

A

Thank you for your help today, Mrs. Lee.

B

You're welcome, David. Have a good evening!

C

I gotta go now. My mom's waiting.

D

Okay, see you tomorrow at school!

Practice Activities

1. Complete the Dialogues

A: "Thanks for the help!"

B: ""

A: "I have to leave now."

B: ""

2. Matching Game

Match each situation with the appropriate farewell:

Leaving a teacher's office
"Goodbye, have a nice day!"
Saying bye to friends
"See ya later!"

3. Create Your Own Dialogues

Cultural Tip

In English-speaking cultures, farewells often include future plans ("See you tomorrow") or well-wishing ("Have a good weekend").

Farewell Customs

  • Handshakes are common in formal situations
  • Hugs may be appropriate between close friends
  • Waving is common for casual goodbyes
  • Leaving without saying goodbye is considered rude
